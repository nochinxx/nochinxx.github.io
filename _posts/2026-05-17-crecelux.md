<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-8 mb-4 border-b-2 border-indigo-200 pb-2"> Overview </h2>

**Crecelux** is a frontend/product interface prototype built with **Next.js** and **Material UI** to explore how multiple tools and content experiences can live under one parent platform without feeling fragmented.

The prototype combines a **responsive landing page**, **product-card layout**, **bilingual navigation**, and a separate **Books by Jorge** reading interface. One of the displayed tools is **GoalsForMe**, which helps frame the landing page as a multi-product surface rather than a single standalone app.

---

<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-8 mb-4 border-b-2 border-indigo-200 pb-2"> Frontend Direction </h2>

<ul class="list-disc ml-6">
  <li><b>Responsive landing page</b> — The main marketing surface uses a clean section structure with a mobile drawer, desktop nav, and flexible spacing that adapts cleanly across breakpoints.</li>
  <li><b>Product-card layout</b> — The landing page presents multiple tools as distinct cards with logos, descriptions, and entry points, including GoalsForMe as one of the visible products.</li>
  <li><b>Bilingual navigation</b> — Locale-aware routing and language toggles support both English and Spanish flows across the interface.</li>
  <li><b>Clean visual hierarchy</b> — The prototype leans on MUI typography, spacing, and card patterns to keep the interface readable without overcomplicating the layout.</li>
</ul>

---

<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-8 mb-4 border-b-2 border-indigo-200 pb-2"> Books by Jorge Interface </h2>

The **Books by Jorge** section extends the prototype beyond a landing page into a content interface. It uses a **reader-friendly layout** with book cards, cover art, localized metadata, and dedicated reading routes so content can feel like a product experience rather than a static document dump.

That split is what makes the prototype interesting: one surface introduces the broader product family, while another shows how a more editorial experience can still live under the same visual system.

---

<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-8 mb-4 border-b-2 border-indigo-200 pb-2"> Stack </h2>

- **Frontend:** Next.js, React, Material UI  
- **Localization:** next-intl with locale-aware routes  
- **Auth/infra:** Firebase  
- **Focus:** product UI, navigation, cards, and content layout

---

<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-8 mb-4 border-b-2 border-indigo-200 pb-2"> Why It Matters </h2>

This prototype focuses on **frontend architecture and interface composition**: how to structure a parent platform, how to present multiple tools clearly, and how to keep content-heavy experiences readable inside the same product family.
